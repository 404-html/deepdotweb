    <p>According to a press release from <a href="https://www.om.nl/actueel/nieuwsberichten/@89582/hechtenis-online/">The Dutch National Public Prosecutor’s</a> office brought the persons who are suspected of running the vendor-accounts <strong>HollandOnline</strong> (<a href="https://www.deepdotweb.com/2015/03/12/dutch-vendor-bust-hollandonline/">who was arrested 3 months ago</a>), <strong>Lowlands</strong>, <strong>AlbertHeijn</strong>, <strong>TheHeineken</strong> and their supplier <strong>AmsterdamUnited</strong> to court yesterday. These accounts were used to sell narcotics on Silk Road 1, Silk Road 2, Evolution and <a href="http://www.deepdotweb.com/marketplace-directory/listing/agora-market">Agora.</a></p>
    <p>In close cooperation with Dutch Police, National High Tech Crime Unit, Dutch Customs, Europol, Eurojust and LEA’s in other countries, the National Public Prosecutor’s Office will continue to identify and prosecute the major (Dutch) vendors on the darknet markets. The court decided to extend the suspects’ pretrial detention for another three months. The next trial date will be September 1st.</p>
